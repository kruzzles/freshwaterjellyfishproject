PARAGRAPHS

SECTION 1

     The species that I will be focusing on for this project is the freshwater jellyfish. They have existed on earth for up to 600 million years, they became an endangered species in 2002. Humans hunt some edible species of jellyfish. Jellyfish is used in dishes like salads and sushi however, scientists have said jellyfish are not a significant item on the seafood menu as they are made up of 95% water and have very little calories. Even though they aren't a very good source of calories there are other possible benefits. Some of these benefits are having important nutrients like protein and antioxidants. Freshwater jellyfish are very sensitive to changes in their environment so human interventions such as pollution, changes in climate and overfishing are very harmful to them. The water temperature freshwater jellyfish need to survive is 20-25°C (68-77°F.) Thermal pollution can increase water temperature which is very dangerous to jellyfish. Another issue pollution has caused for jellyfish is that it is killing phytoplankton which is one of their main prey.

SECTION 2

     There is very little biological diversity in freshwater jellyfish. This is due to a few factors. The first one is that they have very few predators. The second is that freshwater jellyfish almost always reproduce by budding, asexual reproduction makes exact clones which means no variation happens. The third is the impacts humans have had on them, they are very quickly losing their habitat which is causing less biodiversity. Thermal pollution causes more algae to grow and water temperature to increase. When there is too much algae toxins are released into the water, the water's oxygen content is lowered and the sunlight is blocked. When this happens to an ecosystem the jellyfish are likely to die off as they are sensitive to change.

SECTION 3

     There is a medicine called prevagen which uses a protein found in jellyfish called apoaequorin. Prevagen is used to help with memory loss and in 2015 Quincy Bioscience (the company that made prevagen) said prevagen is the "best-selling branded memory suppliment." Unfortunately, there are issues with prevagen. The food and drug administration (FDA) has questioned its safety and the makers of Prevagen were sued by the FTC (federal trade commission) and the New York Attorney General over false claims about the product. Prevagen is made without harming jellyfish at all which also means it has little impact on the environment and doesn't affect the jellyfish.

SECTION 4

     Humans should use biotechnology on jellyfish. There is a lot of potential for new medicines and antibiotics to be created from jellyfish. Prevagen has proved that jellyfish can certainly be used to create helpful products. People should have more involvement in biodiversity in jellyfish, it is clear having no variation has been an issue to freshwater jellyfish. If humans would help freshwater jellyfish be more diverse they would be less sensitive to changes in their environment. There have been many other successes in biotechnology used for jellyfish. Jellyfish venom is being tested for treating diseases. The reason they are testing jellyfish venom is because it can destroy cells, this means it could slow the growth of cells that have a disease. This may seem outrageous but in a lab trial, jellyfish venom was able to stop the growth of cancer cells. It slowed down the growth of certain cancer cells after exposure to venom for 48 hours. The study revealed that the venom affected the development of lung and liver cancer cells. However, these are only tests are there is still a long way to go before we can use jellyfish to treat disease. So far there is little proof that we can treat disease with jellyfish.
